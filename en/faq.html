Hyaluronic acid (HA) fillers are soft, versatile and — crucially — reversible: they can be dissolved with hyaluronidase if needed. Radiesse is a calcium hydroxyapatite filler with biostimulatory properties: it provides immediate volume while also triggering your own collagen production. It tends to last longer and works particularly well for deeper volume loss and structural areas like the jawline or cheeks. Unlike HA fillers, Radiesse cannot be dissolved, so precise technique and careful patient selection matter even more.

PDRN (polydeoxyribonucleotide) is the active ingredient — extracted and purified from salmon DNA, which has a molecular structure very similar to human DNA. The name raises eyebrows, but the science is solid: PDRN has been used in regenerative medicine for decades. In aesthetics, it gained popularity for its results on thin, delicate skin — particularly the under-eye area and neck — where it improves hydration, texture and cellular repair from within.

Biostimulators work gradually — unlike fillers, there's no immediate visible change. Results develop over weeks to months as your skin rebuilds collagen. Different tools, different goals. Fillers add or restore volume immediately. Biostimulators improve the quality and structure of the skin itself. For many patients, combining both gives the best result — and that's often what I'd suggest after seeing your skin in person.
